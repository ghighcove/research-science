## Methodology Notes

**Data source**: FDA FAERS via openFDA API (opendata.fda.gov). Covers voluntary adverse event reports submitted by patients, providers, and manufacturers.

**PRR formula**: `(a / N_drug) / (N_event / N_total)` where a = reports with both drug and event, N_drug = total drug reports, N_event = total event reports, N_total = total FAERS reports.

**Limitations**:
- FAERS captures reports, not causation. High PRR = disproportionate reporting, not proven harm.
- Notoriety bias: widely-publicized effects are over-reported.
- Weber effect: new drugs accumulate reports rapidly in first 2 years.
- Drug name matching is approximate (brand/generic variation in reports).
- Co-medication confounding: patients taking SSRIs typically take other drugs.

**Novel signal interpretation**: Signals not in known SSRI AE profiles warrant clinical pharmacologist review. They may reflect: (1) real under-labeled effects, (2) confounding by co-medications, (3) indication bias, or (4) reporting artifacts.
